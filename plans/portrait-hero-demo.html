Honest take: Option A (current) is probably the right call to keep — here's why:
  • Option B (full 2:3 hero) — the card becomes extremely tall. In a list of entries, each portrait card takes up almost the full screen height. Feels more like a photo gallery than a journal.
  • Option C (4:5 crop) — less extreme, but you're now cropping the top of the portrait photo, which often cuts off the baby's head. Parents won't love that.
  • Option A (current side-by-side) — space-efficient, image and text read as a unit, and portrait images display at their natural proportions inside the column. This is already the better-looking layout, which is why you noticed it. Leave it as-is.

Recommendation: keep portrait cards as side-by-side (Option A). The hero overlay is a good upgrade for landscape, not portrait.
