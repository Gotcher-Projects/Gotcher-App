**Status:** Needs Verification

## Goal
Get the privacy policy production-ready for App Store and Play Store submission: remove the beta banner, clarify camera/photo permission language, restrict distribution to North America (avoiding GDPR requirements), and document the Privacy Nutrition Labels that must be filled out manually in App Store Connect and Play Console.

## Prerequisites (manual — do before this session)
1. **Create privacy@cradlehq.app email** — this address is referenced throughout the privacy policy but does not exist yet. Sign up for [Zoho Mail](https://www.zoho.com/mail/) (free forever, 1 domain, up to 5 users), add the required DNS records to cradlehq.app, and create the privacy@cradlehq.app mailbox. You can log in directly at mail.zoho.com — no forwarding needed.
2. **Verify the privacy policy is live** — confirm `https://cradlehq.app/privacy.html` loads correctly in a browser. This is the URL you'll provide to both Apple and Google during submission.

## Steps

### 1. Remove the beta banner
Remove the `<div class="banner">` block and its associated `.banner` CSS rule from `Frontend/public/privacy.html`. The app is no longer in beta.

### 2. Add camera/photo library permission language
In Section 2 (Information We Collect), add a clarifying bullet under "Photos and images you choose to upload":

> *On mobile devices, CradleHQ requests access to your photo library solely to let you select photos for journal entries and milestone records. We do not access photos you have not explicitly selected.*

This directly answers the question Apple reviewers ask about the `NSPhotoLibraryUsageDescription` permission.

### 3. Update the effective date
Update the "Last Updated" date in the policy header to the date of this session.

## Manual Steps (not code)

### Restrict distribution to North America
In both App Store Connect and Google Play Console, restrict availability to the following countries only: **United States, Canada, Mexico**. This keeps the app out of the EU and eliminates any GDPR obligations. You can expand regions later once the app is established.

- **App Store Connect:** App → Pricing and Availability → select specific countries → uncheck all, then check US, Canada, Mexico
- **Play Console:** Store presence → Countries / regions → select only US, Canada, Mexico

### Privacy Nutrition Labels for Apple
When creating the iOS app record in App Store Connect, you must declare all data types the app collects. Based on CradleHQ's current feature set, fill out the labels as follows:

| Data Type | Collected? | Linked to User? | Used For |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| Email Address | Yes | Yes | Account management |
| Name (display name) | Yes | Yes | App functionality |
| Photos or Videos | Yes | Yes | App functionality |
| User ID | Yes | Yes | App functionality |
| Product Interaction | No | — | — |
| Crash Data | No | — | — |

**Health & Fitness flag:** Feeding logs, sleep records, and diaper tracking *may* be classified as health data by Apple. During the questionnaire, if asked whether the app collects health or fitness data, answer **Yes** and classify it as "Health" data linked to the user, used for app functionality. Answering No and getting flagged during review is worse than over-disclosing.

**No data sold, no advertising data, no tracking** — confirm these as No across the board.

### Data Safety for Google Play
Fill out the Data Safety form in Play Console:
- Data collected: email, name, photos, app activity (feeding/sleep/diaper logs)
- All data is encrypted in transit
- Users can request deletion (via privacy@cradlehq.app)
- No data shared with third parties for advertising
