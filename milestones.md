# Baby Development Milestones (Birth to 18 Months)

## 0-3 Months

### Physical Development
1. Lifts head briefly when lying on stomach
2. Follows objects with eyes
3. Grasps objects reflexively
4. Startles at loud noises
5. Moves arms and legs vigorously
6. Opens and closes hands
7. Brings hands to mouth
8. Holds head at 45-degree angle
9. Supports head for short periods
10. Kicks legs when excited
11. Makes jerky, uncoordinated movements

### Cognitive Development
12. Responds to high-pitched voices
13. Recognizes parent's face and voice
14. Focuses on objects for brief moments
15. Tracks moving objects with eyes
16. Shows preference for certain faces
17. Begins to focus on hands

### Social & Emotional Development
18. Cries to communicate needs
19. Makes eye contact
20. Shows reflex smile
21. Calms when picked up
22. Responds to gentle touch
23. Shows recognition of familiar people

### Communication
24. Makes cooing sounds
25. Cries differently for different needs
26. Makes grunting sounds
27. Listens to voices

## 3-6 Months

### Physical Development
28. Lifts head 90 degrees when on stomach
29. Rolls from front to back
30. Keeps head steady when held upright
31. Bears weight on legs when supported
32. Transfers objects from one hand to another
33. Rakes objects with fingers
34. Reaches for objects
35. Sits with minimal support
36. Balances head when sitting
37. Supports some weight on arms
38. Splashes in bath water

### Cognitive Development
39. Recognizes familiar objects
40. Shows curiosity about surroundings
41. Notices cause and effect (shaking rattle produces sound)
42. Looks for dropped objects
43. Follows moving objects smoothly
44. Shows interest in images in mirrors

### Social & Emotional Development
45. Shows genuine smiles
46. Laughs aloud
47. Shows excitement and delight
48. Reaches for familiar people
49. Shows stranger anxiety beginning
50. Engages in reciprocal play

### Communication
51. Babbles (ba, da, ga sounds)
52. Responds to own name
53. Makes vowel sounds (ah, oh, uh)
54. Imitates sounds
55. Expresses joy with vocalizations
56. Uses voice to express pleasure and displeasure

## 6-9 Months

### Physical Development
57. Sits without support for brief periods
58. Moves from sitting to lying down controlled
59. Crawls forward (army crawl)
60. Crawls on hands and knees
61. Pulls self to standing position
62. Stands while holding onto furniture
63. Picks up small objects with thumb and fingers (pincer grasp)
64. Waves goodbye
65. Claps hands
66. Passes object from hand to hand
67. Bangs objects together
68. Drops objects deliberately to watch them fall

### Cognitive Development
69. Shows understanding of object permanence
70. Recognizes own name consistently
71. Understands "no"
72. Shows memory for events
73. Investigates objects by shaking, banging, dropping
74. Plays peek-a-boo with understanding

### Social & Emotional Development
75. Shows affection for familiar people
76. Shows distinct likes and dislikes
77. Expresses several emotions (joy, fear, frustration)
78. Shows stranger anxiety more pronounced
79. Enjoys being around family
80. Plays alongside (not with) other babies

### Communication
81. Says "mama" or "dada" (not necessarily meaning them)
82. Babbles with more varied sounds
83. Understands simple gestures
84. Uses gestures to communicate
85. Understands tone of voice

## 9-12 Months

### Physical Development
86. Pulls self up to standing position
87. Cruises along furniture
88. May take first steps
89. Stands briefly without support
90. Uses pincer grasp effectively
91. Throws objects intentionally
92. Drinks from cup with assistance
93. Begins self-feeding with foods
94. Climbs on low furniture
95. Walks with one hand held

### Cognitive Development
96. Understands simple words
97. Follows simple one-step directions
98. Shows memory for familiar people and objects
99. Engages in exploration with purpose
100. Shows understanding of simple cause and effect

### Social & Emotional Development
101. Shows increased stranger anxiety
102. May show separation anxiety
103. Shows affection through hugs
104. Enjoys interactive games
105. Shows preference for certain people
106. Shows pride in accomplishments

### Communication
107. Says first true word (mama, dada meaning parent)
108. Uses gestures like pointing
109. Understands simple questions
110. Makes sounds in response to conversation
111. Uses gestures to refuse

## 12-15 Months

### Physical Development
112. Takes first independent steps
113. Walks with support needed
114. Climbs stairs with assistance
115. Bends down to pick up objects
116. Scribbles with crayon
117. Stacks two blocks
118. Throws ball overhand
119. Kicks ball forward
120. Pedals tricycle with help
121. Walks backwards

### Cognitive Development
122. Points to objects when named
123. Explores objects more thoroughly
124. Shows understanding of several words
125. Enjoys looking at picture books
126. Shows pretend play beginning
127. Understands simple instructions

### Social & Emotional Development
128. Imitates actions and words
129. Shows affection openly
130. Shows empathy for others
131. Engages in parallel play
132. Enjoys praise and recognition
133. Shows increased independence

### Communication
134. Says 3-5 words
135. Points and vocalizes
136. Understands 50+ words
137. Responds to name reliably
138. Makes request sounds
139. Uses "no" expressively

## 15-18 Months

### Physical Development
140. Walks independently
141. Runs with stiff legs
142. Climbs stairs with one hand held
143. Sits in small chair
144. Drinks from cup with spilling
145. Uses spoon messily
146. Throws ball in general direction
147. Kicks ball with some control
148. Jumps with both feet (limited height)
149. Walks backward

### Cognitive Development
150. Points to familiar objects by name
151. Shows understanding of 50-100 words
152. Engages in pretend play (feeding doll, pretend cooking)
153. Shows memory for recent events
154. Solves simple problems through trial and error
155. Identifies body parts when named

### Social & Emotional Development
156. Shows definite preferences for people and activities
157. Engages in interactive play with adults
158. Shows possessive behavior with toys
159. Expresses affection demonstratively
160. Shows pride in accomplishments
161. Shows willingness and defiance
162. Enjoys imitating adult behavior

### Communication
163. Says 10-20 words
164. Points to request things
165. Uses gestures along with words
166. Understands simple two-word questions
167. Says "no" frequently
168. Names familiar objects and people
169. Attempts to repeat words spoken
170. Makes animal sounds
171. Uses exclamations like "uh-oh"

---

## Notes on Developmental Milestones

- Every child develops at their own pace
- These milestones represent typical development
- Variations within a few months are normal
- Premature babies should have developmental age adjusted for prematurity during first 2 years
- Environmental factors, genetics, and individual temperament influence developmental timeline
- Consult healthcare provider if significant delays are noticed
